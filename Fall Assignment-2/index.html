History of Jolly Rancher

Jolly Rancher was created in 1949 by Bill and Dorothy Harmsen in Golden, Colorado. Originally, the company sold chocolates and ice cream. However, to stay relevant during the winter months, the Harmsens introduced their now-famous hard candies. Their goal was to provide a “friendly, Western company,” hence the name "Jolly Rancher." Over the decades, Jolly Rancher has grown to be an iconic brand known worldwide for its vibrant flavors and long-lasting sweetness. It has become a staple in candy bowls and trick-or-treat bags everywhere.

What Makes a Jolly Rancher?

Jolly Ranchers are crafted with a unique combination of ingredients, carefully balanced to provide their characteristic bold and tangy taste. Here are the main components:

  • Sugar: The primary ingredient, giving Jolly Ranchers their sweetness.
  • Corn Syrup: Adds to the candy's smooth texture and consistency.
  • Artificial & Natural Flavors: Each flavor – from green apple to watermelon – is crafted with flavor extracts for a powerful punch.
  • Artificial Colors: Bright and fun, the colors of Jolly Ranchers make them as visually appealing as they are delicious!

Fun Facts About Jolly Ranchers

  • Did you know that Jolly Ranchers were originally available in sour apple, grape, cherry, orange, and lemon flavors? Some of these have been retired over the years.
  • The longest Jolly Rancher flavor to disappear was peach, but it returned briefly in limited editions due to popular demand.
  • Each flavor is created to last, making Jolly Ranchers the perfect candy for those who want a long-lasting treat.
  • Jolly Ranchers are also known for their iconic “stickiness,” which makes them a popular choice for melting down and using in recipes or even as part of science experiments!
